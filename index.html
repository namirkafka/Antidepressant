What do these terms mean?
Prefrontal cortex

Helps with planning, focus, decision-making, and slowing yourself down before reacting.

Amygdala

The brain's fast alarm system. It helps detect threat, fear, and emotional urgency.

Hippocampus

Helps with memory, context, and how the brain recovers from long-term stress.

Striatum

Helps with motivation, reward, interest, and the feeling that something is worth doing.

Insula

Tracks internal body signals like tension, discomfort, calm, and emotional intensity.

Effect level

Shows how strongly this simplified model thinks a system may be shifting. It does not mean good or bad by itself.

Serotonin

Often discussed in depression and anxiety treatment. It is commonly linked to mood balance and emotional steadiness.

Norepinephrine

Often linked to alertness, energy, attention, and the body's stress response.

Dopamine

Often linked to reward, motivation, drive, pleasure, and the sense that something feels worth doing.

SSRI

A common antidepressant group that mainly affects serotonin. Examples include sertraline and fluoxetine.

SNRI

An antidepressant group that mainly affects serotonin and norepinephrine. Examples include venlafaxine and duloxetine.

Atypical antidepressants

A mixed group of antidepressants that do not all work the same way. Some may involve dopamine more than SSRIs do.

Tricyclic antidepressants

An older antidepressant group with broader effects across brain signaling systems and often more side effects.
